# Packaging & checkout disclaimers

Copy for bracelet packaging, product pages, and checkout. Not legal advice — have counsel review before high-volume sales.

## Product page / insert (required)

**Emergency medical ID bracelet — not a medical device**

RedMed stores your allergies, medications, and emergency contacts on a **passive NFC chip** in this bracelet (no battery, no broadcast). **A smartphone held within an inch or two of the bracelet** briefly powers the chip and reads that information in its browser — that is intentional so first responders can help you without installing an app. **Not for card readers, payment terminals, active RFID, BLE beacons, or other NFC devices.**

**On tap, responders see Call 911 and trauma-hospital transport options first** — for when someone may not survive if you wait for a closer hospital. Then your medical profile.

- You enter and control your own data.
- Data on the tag is **not encrypted**.
- The bracelet has **no battery** and needs **no charging**.
- RedMed does **not** verify your medical information.
- This product is **not** a medical device and does not diagnose or treat conditions.
- **Call 911** (or your local emergency number) in a life-threatening emergency.

## Setup steps (insert card)

1. Download RedMed (optional) or open [the web app](https://www.redmed.com/).
2. Fill in your profile → **Save**.
3. Write the card link to this bracelet (iOS **Write Tag** or NFC Tools app).
4. Tap the bracelet with your phone to test.

When your information changes, **rewrite the tag**.

## Checkout consent (e-commerce)

Use a required checkbox before payment:

> I understand that medical information I provide may be encoded on an NFC tag that **anyone can read by tapping**, that RedMed does not verify this information, and that the bracelet is **not a medical device**. I agree to the [Terms of Use](https://www.redmed.com/terms-of-service.html) and [Privacy Policy](https://www.redmed.com/privacy-policy.html).

For **pre-encoded** orders, add:

> I consent to RedMed encoding my submitted medical profile onto the NFC bracelet I am purchasing.

## Box / sleeve — short version

**Tap to read.** Unencrypted medical ID for emergencies. Not FDA-approved. Not medical advice.

Suggested policy: replace defective tags/bracelets that fail NDEF read/write QA; customer responsible for re-encoding after profile changes.
